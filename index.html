What is CDH Studio?

The format of this 16-week internship program acts as a sandbox for talented and innovative interns to reach their full potential. The Program creates teams of 3 students to work independently on an experimental project for a project sponsor through our CDH Studio.

Teams will be paired with volunteer mentors allowing students and mentors to learn and grow together. This will enable and encourage bidirectional knowledge transfer between the students and mentors.

What is ISED?

CDH Studio is an elite internship program organized by Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED). ISED works with Canadians in all areas of the economy and in all parts of the country to improve conditions for investment, enhance Canada's innovation performance, increase Canada's share of global trade and build a fair, efficient and competitive marketplace.

CDH Studio is located at ISED HQ inside Ottawa's iconic C.D. Howe Building.



Ottawa

Who is C.D. Howe?

CDH is named after influential Canadian politician and engineer, C.D. Howe. During his time in office, he modernized many Canadian industries and took part in establishing Canadian icons such as CBC and Air Canada. We hope to bring his inspiration, drive, and innovation to our projects and students.

The Project

Theme

The theme for winter 2019 is data analytics, machine learning and chatbots. Selected students will work in a team to propose and implement an idea within the time and theme provided.

Interns will be responsible for scoping their project, building it from scratch, and working with sponsor stakeholders, and mentors along the way

The team will demo its product to the project sponsor, the executive team, and other full-time employees at the end of the internship.

Timeline

The proposed timeline of the project will be as follows:

  • Pick a Project

    Students select their project on a pre-selected topic related to IT renewal

  • Project Proposal

    Students prepare a project proposal

    • describe the idea
    • divide out tasks and responsibilities
    • create project timeline
    • plan 2-week sprints

  • Code!

    Students must document work, communicate within their team, seek help, and find resources

  • Present the Project

    Students prepare a formal presentation and present their project to DSD Management.

    • answer questions
    • defend choices
    • identify lessons learned

  • All Done!
